Enzo Ferrari

Race Driver | Founder of Ferrari

Time Line (1898-1988)

  • 1898: Born in Modena, Italy.
  • 1908: Watched Felice Nazzaro's win at the Circuit de Bologna, which inspired him to become a car racer.
  • 1916: Assigned to the Italian Army during World War I.
  • 1918: Became severely sick due to the Italian flu outbreak, discharged from Italian army service.
  • 1919: Settled with a job at C.M.N, an Italian car company and represented them in the Parma-Poggio di Berceto hillclimb race. This was his first race and he finished fourth in the three-litre category at the wheel of a 2.3-lit.
  • 1920: Left C.M.N to race for Alfa Romeo, a well known race company at the time.
  • 1932: Form his own race team called Sculderia Ferrari, but it's still being a branching team from Alfa Romeo to receive support.
  • 1933: Alpha Romeo ran into financial struggles and stopped funding Ferrari's race team which left the team on their own.
  • 1937: Alpha Romeo wanted to regain full control of Ferrari's race team making Enzo Ferrari a sporting director. Ferrari was unhappy and left Alpha Romeo to start his own company Auto-Avio Costruzioni to supply parts to other racing teams.
  • 1943: During the outbreak of World War II, Ferrari's factory got bombed and destroyed. He relocated from Modena to Maranello.
  • 1947: Founded Ferrari S.p.A, making and using cars bearing his name.
  • 1949: S.p.A's first major victory in an race with a Ferrari 166M driven by Luigi Chinetti.
  • 1950: Enrolled in the new formula 1 world championship and finished within the top 10.
  • 1951: Ferrari won his first Grand Prix with Jose Froilan Gonzalez at Silverstone.
  • 1952: Ferrari won his first championship with Alberto Ascari.
  • 1957: An accident occurred during a race causing the death of his co-driver and 9 spectators, he was charged with manslaughter in a lengthy prosecution for 4 years.
  • 1964: Ferrari designed the Ferrari 250 GTO which sold for 36 million dollar in 2013 making it the most expensive car in the world.
  • 1969: Increasing financial difficulties caused Enzo Ferrari to start looking for a business partner for fund his race team. Fiat purchased 50% of his team and gave funding support. He can still take full control of the team
  • 1971: Ferrari stepped down and let Fiat in charge of the road car portion (designing/selling) while Ferrari still focused on the racing portion.
  • 1974: Ferrari stepped down from the race car portion and appointed Luca Cordero di Montezemolo as Sporting Director/Formula One Team manager.
  • 1988: Ferrari died at the age of 90.
